/about

who we actually are.

The Computer Science Honor Society exists for one simple reason: building things with code is more fun with other people. We're a student-run chapter where members of all skill levels come together to learn, collaborate, and share what we know.

Our weekly meetings cover everything from algorithms and competitive programming to web development, machine learning, cybersecurity, and game design. We run workshops for newcomers, organize hackathons, host guest speakers from industry, and compete in regional and national competitions.

Beyond the technical side, CSHS members volunteer as peer tutors, mentor students in introductory CS classes, and help organize Hour of Code events at local elementary and middle schools. We believe computer science is a craft worth sharing — and that the best way to learn is to teach.

If you're considering joining, don't worry about being "good enough." We have members who placed at state finals and members who are still learning what a for-loop is. Both are welcome. The only requirement is curiosity.

/principles
01
Curiosity over credentials
We care about whether you want to learn, not whether you already know. Every senior in this club was a beginner once.
02
Build, don't just consume
Watching tutorials is fine. Shipping something — even something small and ugly — is better. We ship.
03
Teach what you learn
The fastest way to understand something is to explain it to someone else. Tutoring is part of how we grow.
04
Hard problems, soft people
We attack tough problems together and we're kind to each other while we do it. Both halves matter.
/programs

What we actually do

01 // WEEKLY

General meetings

Every week we get together to talk about a new topic — could be a deep dive on a CS concept, a project demo from a member, or a collaborative coding session.

02 // QUARTERLY

Hackathons

Build-anything events ranging from a few hours to a full weekend. Teams form on-site, prizes go to the most creative and the most ambitious projects.

03 // MONTHLY

Skill workshops

Hands-on sessions on specific tools and topics: Git, Linux, web frameworks, ML libraries, embedded systems, whatever members are curious about.

04 // ONGOING

Peer tutoring

Members volunteer time to help students in AP CS and intro classes. Counts toward honor society service hours.

05 // SEASONAL

Competitions

We send teams to CyberPatriot, USACO, regional programming contests, and the occasional Capture the Flag event. Practice sessions before each one.

06 // ROLLING

Guest speakers

We invite engineers, researchers, and CS students from local universities to talk about what they actually do. Always followed by Q&A.
